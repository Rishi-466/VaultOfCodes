Fresh Fir Needle Salad

A refreshing salad that captures the essence of the forest with young, tender fir needles.

Ingredients:
  • 1 cup young fir needles (new growth), finely chopped
  • 4 cups mixed salad greens
  • 1/2 cup walnuts, toasted
  • 1/4 cup dried cranberries
  • 1/4 cup crumbled goat cheese
  • 1 small shallot, thinly sliced
For the dressing:
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp honey
  • 1/2 tsp Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper to taste
Instructions:
  1. In a large bowl, combine the fir needles, salad greens, walnuts, cranberries, goat cheese, and shallot.
  2. In a small jar, combine all dressing ingredients and shake well to emulsify.
  3. Drizzle the dressing over the salad and toss gently to combine.
  4. Serve immediately and enjoy the fresh forest flavors!
Tip: Only use the bright green new growth fir needles as they're more tender and less resinous than older needles.
Healing Fir Needle Tea

A soothing herbal tea rich in vitamin C and antioxidants, perfect for cold season.

Ingredients:
  • 1/2 cup fresh fir needles (washed)
  • 4 cups water
  • 1-2 tbsp honey (optional)
  • 1 cinnamon stick (optional)
  • 1 tsp lemon juice (optional)
Instructions:
  1. Gently rinse the fir needles under cool water to remove any debris.
  2. Bring water to a boil in a small saucepan.
  3. Add the fir needles and cinnamon stick (if using), reduce heat to low, and simmer for 10 minutes.
  4. Remove from heat and let steep for an additional 5 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea into cups, add honey and lemon juice if desired, and enjoy warm.
Health Benefits: Fir needle tea is naturally high in vitamin C (containing 3-5 times more than oranges) and has been traditionally used to support respiratory health.
Fir Tip Honey

A sweet and aromatic honey infused with the essence of young fir tips, perfect for teas, desserts, or as a natural remedy.

Ingredients:
  • 1 cup fresh fir tips (new growth)
  • 2 cups raw honey
  • 1 clean glass jar with lid
Instructions:
  1. Gently rinse the fir tips and pat them dry with a clean towel.
  2. Place the fir tips in the glass jar, pressing them down slightly.
  3. Warm the honey slightly (just enough to make it pourable, not hot) and pour it over the fir tips, ensuring they're completely submerged.
  4. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-4 weeks, shaking gently every few days.
  5. After infusing, strain out the fir tips if desired, or leave them in for continued flavor development.
  6. Store in a cool place and use within a year.
Usage Ideas: Stir into tea, drizzle over yogurt or ice cream, use as a glaze for meats, or take by the spoonful when you feel a cold coming on.
Fir Needle Syrup

A versatile syrup that captures the essence of the forest, perfect for cocktails, desserts, or as a natural remedy.

Ingredients:
  • 2 cups fresh fir needles (packed)
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 cups sugar
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
Instructions:
  1. Rinse the fir needles and pat dry.
  2. In a saucepan, bring water to a boil. Add fir needles, remove from heat, cover, and let steep for 30 minutes.
  3. Strain the liquid through a fine mesh sieve or cheesecloth, pressing to extract all liquid.
  4. Return the liquid to the saucepan and add sugar and lemon juice.
  5. Bring to a gentle boil, stirring until sugar dissolves, then reduce heat and simmer for 15-20 minutes until slightly thickened.
  6. Let cool, then bottle in sterilized jars or bottles. Store in refrigerator for up to 3 months.
Serving Suggestions: Use in cocktails (especially with gin or vodka), drizzle over pancakes or waffles, mix into sparkling water, or take by the spoonful as a winter remedy.
Fir-infused Salt

A fragrant finishing salt that adds a touch of forest flavor to any dish, from roasted meats to chocolate desserts.

Ingredients:
  • 1 cup fresh fir needles (new growth preferred)
  • 1 cup coarse sea salt or kosher salt
Instructions:
  1. Rinse fir needles and pat dry thoroughly.
  2. Finely chop the needles (a food processor works well for this).
  3. Mix the chopped needles with the salt in a bowl.
  4. Spread the mixture on a baking sheet and let dry at room temperature for 2-3 days, stirring occasionally.
  5. Alternatively, you can dry in a dehydrator or oven at the lowest setting (under 150°F) for 1-2 hours.
  6. Once completely dry, store in an airtight container. Will keep for 6-12 months.
Usage Ideas: Sprinkle on roasted vegetables, rim cocktail glasses, season grilled meats, or even use on chocolate desserts for an unexpected flavor pairing.
Fir Shortbread Cookies

Buttery, crumbly shortbread cookies with a subtle piney flavor from finely chopped fir needles.

Ingredients:
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 2 tbsp fresh fir needles, finely chopped (new growth)
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Optional: 1/2 tsp lemon zest
Instructions:
  1. Preheat oven to 325°F (160°C).
  2. In a large bowl, cream together butter and sugar until light and fluffy.
  3. Add chopped fir needles, vanilla, and lemon zest (if using), mixing well.
  4. Gradually add flour and salt, mixing until just combined.
  5. Form dough into a log about 2 inches in diameter, wrap in plastic, and chill for 30 minutes.
  6. Slice into 1/4-inch thick rounds and place on parchment-lined baking sheets.
  7. Bake for 15-18 minutes until edges are just beginning to turn golden.
  8. Let cool on baking sheets for 5 minutes, then transfer to wire racks to cool completely.
Serving Suggestion: These pair wonderfully with the fir needle tea or a cup of earl grey. For extra flair, sprinkle with fir-infused salt before baking.
