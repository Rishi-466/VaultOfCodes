Gourmet Mushroom Risotto

Creamy Italian rice dish with wild mushrooms and white wine

This authentic mushroom risotto recipe comes straight from Northern Italy. With its creamy texture, earthy mushroom flavor, and perfect al dente bite, it's comfort food at its finest. The secret is in the slow addition of broth and constant stirring to release the rice's starches.

Instructions

1

Prepare the Ingredients

Heat the broth in a saucepan and keep it warm over low heat. Clean the mushrooms with a damp cloth and slice them. Finely dice the shallot and mince the garlic. Measure out all other ingredients.

2

Sauté the Mushrooms

Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the mushrooms and cook until they release their moisture and become golden brown, about 8-10 minutes. Season with salt and pepper, then transfer to a plate.

3

Cook the Aromatics

In the same skillet, melt 1 tbsp butter with 1 tbsp olive oil over medium heat. Add the shallot and cook until translucent, about 3 minutes. Add the garlic and cook for 30 seconds until fragrant.

4

Toast the Rice

Add the Arborio rice to the skillet and stir to coat with the oil and butter. Cook for 2-3 minutes until the rice becomes slightly translucent around the edges.

5

Deglaze with Wine

Pour in the white wine and stir constantly until it's completely absorbed by the rice, about 2 minutes. The alcohol will evaporate, leaving behind its flavor.

6

Add Broth Gradually

Add the warm broth one ladleful at a time, stirring constantly and allowing each addition to be absorbed before adding the next. This process should take about 20 minutes. The rice should be al dente - tender with a slight bite.

7

Finish the Risotto

Stir in the cooked mushrooms, Parmesan cheese, remaining butter, and cream (if using). Season with salt and pepper to taste. The risotto should be creamy and slightly loose - it will thicken as it stands.

8

Serve Immediately

Garnish with chopped parsley and extra Parmesan cheese. Serve immediately in warm bowls. Risotto waits for no one - it's best enjoyed right after cooking!

Chef's Pro Tips

Broth Temperature: Keep your broth warm throughout the cooking process. Adding cold broth will lower the temperature of the rice and affect the cooking time and texture.

Stirring Technique: Stir constantly but gently. You want to release the starches from the rice to create creaminess, but not so vigorously that you break the grains.

Rice Quality: Never rinse Arborio rice before cooking - you want to preserve the surface starch that creates the creamy texture.

Resting Time: Let the risotto rest for 2 minutes after the final addition of cheese and butter before serving. This allows the flavors to meld perfectly.
